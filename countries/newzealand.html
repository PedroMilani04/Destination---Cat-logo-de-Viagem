New Zealand

Aotearoa [aɔˈtɛaɾɔa] | Area: 268,021 km² | Population: 5,129,290 | Time Zone: UTC+12 & UTC+13

The islands of New Zealand were the last large habitable landmass to be settled by humans. Between about 1280 and 1350, Polynesians began to settle in the islands and then developed a distinctive Māori culture. In 1642, the Dutch explorer Abel Tasman became the first European to sight and record New Zealand.
